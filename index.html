Research Interests
My research areas are computer architecture and high-performance computing (HPC), focusing on building reliable and energy-efficient computer systems from multiple disciplines (e.g., architecture, compiler, and application).

 

Current Research Projects
Soft-Error Protection for GPGPUs. Soft-errors cause transient data corruptions during program execution, but they do not permanently damage the computer system. GPUs are widely used platform for general-purpose computing (also called GPGPUs), which usually have rigorous reliability requirements on execution correctness. As a consequence, GPGPUs are especially vulnerable to soft errors. The goal of this project is to effectively and efficiently protect GPGPUs against soft errors by leveraging the unique characteristic of architecture and applications.

Hardware-Variability aware Computer System Design. As technology scales down, hardware variability (including process, temperature, voltage variations) causes great challenges for computer systems. Chip designers usually sacrifice performance and power for functional correctness in order to mitigate the impact of hardware variability. The goal of this project is to design energy-efficient computer systems in the presence of hardware-variability effects.

Domain-Specific Architecture Design. Domain-specific processors are designed to efficiently process certain application(s). They achieve high energy-efficiency comparing with tradition processors like CPUs. This project is to design domain-specific processors for emerging applications, including artificial intelligence (AI) and Internet-of-Things (IoT).

Approximate Computing for HPC applications. Not all HPC applications require 100% execution correctness, e.g., machine learning, image processing, etc. Approximate computing achieves performance improvement and energy saving by reducing computation accuracy. In this project, we target at improving the performance of HPC applications by leveraging their inherent approximation opportunities.

For Prospective Students
Basic requirements: be familiar with Linux, C/C++ programming, and script languages (e.g., Python); have taken several of the following undergraduate-level courses: computer architecture, computer system organization, compilers, assembly language, and operating systems.

About computer architecture research: computer architecture is the art of designing and implementing computer systems, so as to meet the requirements of performance, functionality, power, energy, reliability, cost, and so on. Here are several popular research topics: memory-hierarchy design, parallelism, network-on-chip, scheduling, and specialized processor design (e.g., machine learning and Internet of things). If you would like to know the recent advances in this field, you can check the top international conferences like ISCA, MICRO, and HPCA. These conferences are highly competitive with usually less than 20% acceptance rate.
